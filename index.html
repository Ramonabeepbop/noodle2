Here's the ultimate guide to every type of noodle!

Explore our diverse selection of noodles, each with its own special qualities!

Ramen (ラーメン)

Wheat flour noodles, firm and chewy, commonly served in a flavorful broth with various toppings.

Udon (うどん)

Thick, chewy wheat flour noodles, usually served in a hot broth or stir-fried.

Soba (そば)

Buckwheat noodles, firm with a slightly nutty flavor, served hot or cold.

Somen (そうめん)

Thin, delicate wheat noodles, typically served chilled with dipping sauce

Yakisoba (焼きそば)

Stir-fried wheat noodles, similar to ramen, with vegetables, meat, and a sweet-savory sauce.

Shirataki (しらたき)

Translucent, gelatinous noodles made from konjac yam, commonly used in hot pots.

Tokoroten (ところてん)

Firm, jelly-like noodles made from agar, usually served cold with a vinegar-based sauce or sweet syrup.

Houtou (ほうとう)

Flat, wide wheat noodles, stewed with vegetables in a miso-based soup, especially popular in Yamanashi Prefecture.
